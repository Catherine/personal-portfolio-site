What do I do?

I am both a developer and a designer. I work to learn how users interact with technology in order to build/improve interfaces and develop seamless experiences. You'll usually find me looking at an Adobe product or my wonderful text editor.

Why do I do it?

Designers and engineers often have very different ways of thinking about and working through problems. As an individual that works on both sides, one of my goals is to help bridge the communication gaps that exist between them in the workplace.

Involvement

I am currently the Tech Director for Scout, Northeastern's student run design studio, and working as a Fellow for the College of Computer and Information Science (CCIS). Previously, I have acted as the President of CCIS's group to support women in computing, nuWiT. In my free time you can find me behind a camera, working on misc. projects, painting, and baking (though probably not all at the same time).

Social Proof Widget

Hypothesis: When users hear of or see other people using a product to solve needs similar to them, they are more willing to use the same product.

What we did: Designed and created two front-end widgets (AB tested) using HTML5, Javascript, and CSS using Design for Delight methodologies. Social posts were pulled from Twitter, HouseParty, and our reviews using a back-end Java service with JAX-RS and AWS Database.

Web Design and Development

This was my final project for IM 4100 Web Design and Development during the fall of 2013. The class emphasized tools and strategies for design, such as site maps, wireframes, prototypes, usability testing, and iterative development.

My project explored parallax scrolling, single-page navigation challenges and solutions, designing images and assets, and creating an online educational experience. Can be found at: catherinepatchell.com/wd

PrereqMe

Planning out courses is frustrating and tedious. My team built a web application called PrereqMe to solve this problem and improve the process while taking CCIS's Human Computer Interaction course. Our product was able to help students visually view prerequisites, provide convenient access to course descriptions, and help them plan for the future.
